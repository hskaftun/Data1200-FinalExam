What can you do as an individual to help tackle the problem of mis- and disinformation on the internet, both in terms of your personal and your professional life?

Over the past years it has been common with fake news online. An example of this can be the misinformation being published about covid-19. But how is someone supposed to understand whether it is fake news or further spreading of previously stated news?

To begin with we must be critical no matter what we read online. There is a large amount of positivity coming from social media, however, one negative side is everyone has a platform which has a wide reach. We must be critical especially to topics which interest us due to the fact that people can use these platforms to write misinformation. It is at this point it is wise to check where these facts are coming from. Is this from a blog or from a newspaper?

Another thing which can be easy to forget is when has what you are reading been written? The information could have been true before, but the information can have changed over time with new applied research.

There are many things you can do so you do not give into fake news, whether it is private or in a job environment. A good rule to remember is to be critical since everyone can publish words online. Being critical will ultimately lead to fake news being read and distributed further.
